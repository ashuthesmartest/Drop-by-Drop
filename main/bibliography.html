          References

          Have a look on some recommended sites:

          Drought for kids
          This site is produced by the National Drought Mitigation Center at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. The National Drought Mitigation Center helps people and governments reduce their risk from drought. This site raises awareness about basics of droughts and mitigation of droughts to children.

          Water Use it Wisely
          A wise website providing tips and awareness to the people in order to save water. Water – Use It Wisely is recognized as a worldwide leader in communicating the simplicity and effectiveness of everyday water-saving behaviors and devices. Now in its eleventh year, the campaign has grown to become the largest, most comprehensive water conservation program in North America.

          Save the Water
          Save the Water™ (STW™) is a U.S.-based non-profit organization dedicated to solving the world water crisis through excellence in water science and by forming alliances with organizations, scientists, universities, media, businesses, and governments around the world to promote awareness of water contamination issues.

          Rain Water Harvesting Organization
          Sometimes, you have to grab an existing problem by the scruff of its neck. It will cringe and cry. It will bring tears to your eyes. Give it a hard shake. To its demands, say "NO!"
          In this way, you stop focusing on the problem, and move on to the solution. You say: "YES!"
          You move from "NO!" to "YES!". From despair to problem-solving. From now to the future.
          This is what the CSE water campaign is about. Call it a politics of hope.
